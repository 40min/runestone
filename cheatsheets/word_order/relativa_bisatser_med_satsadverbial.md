# 🔍 Relativa bisatser med satsadverbial
## Relative Clauses with Sentence Adverbials

---

## 🌟 The Star: **som**

**Som** is your relative pronoun — it connects two clauses like a grammatical matchmaker. 💘

---

## 📌 Rule #1 — som = Subject
> No extra subject follows. **som** IS the subject.

**Läraren fokuserade på barn som inte kunde läsa.**
*The teacher focused on children who couldn't read.*

➡️ **som** = who/that AND the subject of the clause. It's doing double duty. Overachiever. 🏆

---

## 📌 Rule #2 — som = Object
> A new subject sneaks in right after **som**.

**Det fanns en lärare som jag inte tyckte om.**
*There was a teacher that I didn't like.*

➡️ Here **som** = object, and **jag** is the actual subject. **som** just opens the door. 🚪

---

## ⚡ The Golden Rule — Adverbial Placement

Sentence adverbials (**inte, aldrig, alltid, ofta, verkligen**, etc.) go **BEFORE the first verb** in the relative clause.

| ✅ Correct                              | ❌ Wrong                                |
| -------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------- |
| barn som **inte** kunde läsa           | barn som kunde **inte** läsa           |
| en lärare som jag **aldrig** tyckte om | en lärare som jag tyckte **aldrig** om |

> The adverb is like an eager student — it always jumps to the front of the line. 🙋

---

## 🧠 Quick Summary

| **som** role | What follows   | Example                             |
| ------------ | -------------- | ----------------------------------- |
| 👑 Subject    | Verb directly  | *barn som **inte** kunde läsa*      |
| 📦 Object     | Subject + Verb | *lärare som jag **inte** tyckte om* |

### 🪄 Adverbial cheat list
`inte` • `aldrig` • `alltid` • `ofta` • `verkligen` • `redan` • `bara`
→ All of these go **before the first verb**. No exceptions. They are bossy like that. 😤
