# ⏰ Swedish Time Prepositions: *för…sedan*, *om*, *i*, *på*, *per*

> 🧠 **The golden question to ask yourself:** *When did it happen? When will it happen? How long? How fast? How often?*

---

## 1️⃣ Something happened in the past → **för … sedan**

**Form:** `för` + time expression + `sedan`

> 💀 Think: "That's ancient history, bro. It happened **ago**."

| Swedish | English |
|---------|---------|
| *Jag kom till Sverige **för 3 år sedan**.* | I came to Sweden 3 years ago. |
| *Jag åt frukost **för 2 timmar sedan**.* | I ate breakfast 2 hours ago. |
| *Jag var i Göteborg **för 3 veckor sedan**.* | I was in Gothenburg 3 weeks ago. |
| *Jag kom **för 5 månader sedan**.* | I came 5 months ago. |

✅ **Meaning:** Something happened. It's done. Gone. Finished. История. 📜

---

## 2️⃣ Something will happen in the future → **om**

**Form:** `om` + time expression

> 🔮 Think: "I'll do it **in** [X time]… promise." *(Probably won't.)*

| Swedish | English |
|---------|---------|
| *Jag åker hem **om 5 minuter**.* | I'm leaving in 5 minutes. |
| *Jag ringer dig **om 2 timmar**.* | I'll call you in 2 hours. |
| *Jag är framme **om 1 minut**.* | I'll be there in 1 minute. |
| *Jag går på semester **om 3 dagar**.* | I'm going on holiday in 3 days. |

✅ **Meaning:** Something hasn't happened yet — it will happen after this much time from **right now**.

---

## 3️⃣ How long has something been going on? → **i** / **på**

### 3a) Duration (positive) → **i**

> ⏳ "I've been doing this for a while… send help."

| Swedish | English |
|---------|---------|
| *Jag har bott här **i 2 år**.* | I have lived here for 2 years. |
| *Jag har arbetat som lärare **i 11 år**.* | I've worked as a teacher for 11 years. |
| *Jag stannar i Stockholm **i en vecka**.* | I'm staying in Stockholm for a week. |

### 3b) Duration (negative) → **inte … på**

> 🚭 "Haven't done it in ages. Zero. Zilch. Totally clean." *(Sure, Jan.)*

**Form:** `inte` + verb + `på` + time expression

| Swedish | English |
|---------|---------|
| *Jag har **inte** rökt **på 3 år**.* | I haven't smoked for 3 years. |
| *Jag har **inte** sett henne **på en månad**.* | I haven't seen her for a month. |
| *Jag har **inte** sett henne **på länge**.* | I haven't seen her for a long time. |

> ⚠️ **Trap!** Positive → `i`. Negative → `på`. Don't mix them up or Anastasia will sigh. 😬

---

## 4️⃣ How long does it take to finish something? → **på**

**Form:** do something + `på` + time expression

> 🏎️ Think: "I demolished that task **in** [record time]. *Vroom.*"

| Swedish | English |
|---------|---------|
| *Man åker från Göteborg till Stockholm **på 5 timmar**.* | You travel from Gothenburg to Stockholm in 5 hours. |
| *Pedro lärde sig svenska **på ett år**.* | Pedro learnt Swedish in one year. |
| *Jag tvättade bilen **på en halvtimme**.* | I washed the car in half an hour. |
| *Jag städar köket **på en timme**.* | I clean the kitchen in one hour. |
| *Linda lärde sig simma **på 5 dagar**.* | Linda learnt to swim in 5 days. |

✅ **Meaning:** Total time needed to **complete** something from start to finish.

---

## 5️⃣ How often? → **om** / **i** / **per**

> 🔁 "Frequency. Like your coffee habit, but let's not judge."

### 5a) **om** → used with *dagen*, *året*, *dygnet*

| Swedish | English |
|---------|---------|
| *Jag dricker kaffe två gånger **om dagen**.* | I drink coffee twice a day. |
| *Jag har semester två gånger **om året**.* | I go on holiday twice a year. |

### 5b) **i** → used with *veckan*, *månaden*, *timmen*, *minuten*, *sekunden*

| Swedish | English |
|---------|---------|
| *Jag simmar en gång **i veckan**.* | I swim once a week. |
| *Hon kollar sin mobil tre gånger **i minuten**.* | She checks her phone three times a minute. |

### 5c) **per** → same as **i**, but more formal / universal 🧑‍💼

| Swedish | English |
|---------|---------|
| *Hon kollar sin mobil tre gånger **per minut**.* | She checks her phone three times per minute. |
| *Jag simmar en gång **per vecka**.* | I swim once per week. |
| *Jag dricker kaffe två gånger **per dag**.* | I drink coffee twice per day. |

> 💡 **i** vs **per**: same meaning, *i* is more colloquial, *per* sounds slightly more official. Both are fine!

---

## 🧠 Quick Cheat Table

| Question | Preposition | Example |
|----------|-------------|---------|
| **När?** (past) | **för … sedan** | *för 3 år sedan* |
| **När?** (future) | **om** | *om 5 minuter* |
| **Hur länge?** (positive) | **i** | *i 2 år* |
| **Hur länge?** (negative) | **inte … på** | *inte rökt på 3 år* |
| **Hur lång tid tar det?** | **på** | *på en timme* |
| **Hur ofta?** (dag/år/dygn) | **om** | *om dagen, om året* |
| **Hur ofta?** (vecka/månad/timme…) | **i** / **per** | *i veckan, per dag* |
