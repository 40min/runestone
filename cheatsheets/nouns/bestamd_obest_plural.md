# Swedish Nouns Cheatsheet

## The 4 Forms Every Noun Has

| Form | en-word | ett-word |
|------|---------|----------|
| **Indefinite Singular** (a/an) | en katt | ett hus |
| **Definite Singular** (the) | katt**en** | hus**et** |
| **Indefinite Plural** (cats/houses) | katt**er** | hus |
| **Definite Plural** (the cats/houses) | katt**erna** | hus**en** |

---

## Singular Forms

### Indefinite → Definite

| Gender | Add | Example |
|--------|-----|---------|
| **en-words** | **-en** (or -n after vowel) | en bil → bil**en** • en penna → penna**n** |
| **ett-words** | **-et** (or -t after vowel) | ett bord → bord**et** • ett äpple → äpple**t** |

---

## Plural Forms (Indefinite)

### EN-words

| Ending | Rule | Examples |
|--------|------|----------|
| **-or** | Ends in -a | en gata → gat**or** • en flicka → flick**or** |
| **-ar** | Ends in -e, -el, -er, -en, -ing | en bulle → bull**ar** • en cykel → cykl**ar** • en tidning → tidning**ar** |
| **-er** | Stress on last syllable; -an, -ion, -ör, -ist | en mobil → mobil**er** • en student → student**er** • en frisör → frisör**er** |
| **-r** | One syllable + long vowel | en sko → sko**r** • en ko → ko**r** |
| **no change** | Professions in -are, -iker | en lärare → lärare • en elektriker → elektriker |

### ETT-words

| Ending | Rule | Examples |
|--------|------|----------|
| **-n** | Ends in vowel | ett äpple → äpple**n** • ett kvitto → kvitto**n** |
| **no change** | Ends in consonant | ett hus → hus • ett barn → barn |

---

## Definite Plural

**Take indefinite plural + add:**

| Plural ending | Add | Example |
|---------------|-----|---------|
| -or, -ar, -er, -r | **-na** | bilar → bilar**na** • studenter → studenter**na** |
| -n (ett-words) | **-a** | äpplen → äpple**na** |
| no ending | **-en** | hus → hus**en** • barn → barn**en** |

## Essential Tips

✅ **Always learn nouns with en/ett** – you must memorize the gender
✅ **Most en-words** take -or, -ar, -er, or -r in plural
✅ **Most ett-words** take -n or stay unchanged in plural
✅ **Irregular nouns** are very common – memorize them separately
