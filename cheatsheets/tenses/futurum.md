# ⏩ Presens Futurum — Talking About the Future (3 ways!)

---

## 1️⃣ ska + infinitiv — *"I decided this!"*
> Someone is **planning, wanting, or in charge.** Very intentional energy. 💪

**Jag ska studera svenska i Vilnius.**
*I'm going to study Swedish in Vilnius.* (I planned this!)

**Det ska bli varmare i morgon.**
*It's supposed to get warmer tomorrow.* (heard it on the news 📺)

➡️ Use **ska** when:
- **Subject is in control** → making plans, decisions, expressing will
- **Second-hand info** → you're repeating what someone else said or wrote

---

## 2️⃣ Presensform — *"It's basically already happening"*
> Swedish says: why invent a future tense when context does the job? 😎

**Om två år är jag färdig med min examen.**
*In two years I'll be done with my degree.*

**Kursen börjar på måndag.**
*The course starts on Monday.*

➡️ Use **present tense** for future when:
- There's a **time expression** (i morgon, nästa vecka, om två år...) — context makes it obvious
- It's a **time clause with "när"** — Swedish law, no exceptions 🚔

**När jag är klar med läxan ska jag gå ut.**
*When I'm done with my homework, I'll go out.*
> ⚠️ "när" clause = always present tense. Even if everything else is future. Deal with it.

---

## 3️⃣ kommer att + infinitiv — *"This will happen, not my fault"*
> Nobody decided this. It's just... **logic. Nature. Consequences.** 🌩️

**Vilka svarta moln! Det kommer att börja regna.**
*Look at those clouds! It's going to start raining.*

**Om du har i mer chili kommer soppan att bli jättestark.**
*If you add more chili, the soup will be extremely spicy.* (cause & effect 🌶️)

➡️ Use **kommer att** when:
- It's a **natural process** or **logical consequence**
- Nobody planned it — it just *will happen*

> 💬 **Informal tip:** In casual speech, you can drop the **att**:
> *Det kommer bli tufft.* = totally fine at the dinner table 🍽️

---

## 🧠 Quick Cheat Table

| Form                 | Vibe                    | Key trigger                           |
| -------------------- | ----------------------- | ------------------------------------- |
| **ska + inf**        | 💪 "I planned this"      | Subject decides / second-hand info    |
| **Presens**          | 🗓️ "Obviously soon"      | Time expression / när-clause          |
| **kommer att + inf** | 🌦️ "It just will happen" | Natural process / logical consequence |

---

## 🚨 The "när" Rule — Tattoo This On Your Hand

> In time clauses starting with **när**, always use **present tense** — even when talking about the future.

| ❌ Wrong                    | ✅ Right                  |
| -------------------------- | ------------------------ |
| *När jag ska vara klar...* | *När jag **är** klar...* |

Swedish: "Future? In MY när-clause? I don't think so." 😤
