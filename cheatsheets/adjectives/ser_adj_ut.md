# 👀 Ser + adjektiv + ut — Looking Like Something

---

## 🌟 The Construction
> "to look / to seem..." — judging things by appearance since forever 👁️

`subjekt + ser + adjektiv + ut`

---

## 1️⃣ Basic usage — adjective agrees with subject

> The adjective behaves just like normal — it has to match its subject. No freelancing. 📏

| Subject                | Example                          | Adjective form |
| ---------------------- | -------------------------------- | -------------- |
| En mäklare (en-word)   | Mäklaren ser sympatisk **ut**.   | base form      |
| Allt (ett-word/neuter) | Allt ser trevlig**t** ut.        | **-t**         |
| De (plural)            | Objekten ser intressant**a** ut. | **-a**         |

➡️ Same rules as always — **en → base, ett → -t, plural → -a**

---

## 2️⃣ Komparation with ser...ut — Yes, you can compare looks 👀

**Kajsa ser äldre ut än Mia.**
*Kajsa looks older than Mia.*

**De ser lika gamla ut.**
*They look equally old.*

➡️ Just slot in the comparative/superlative adjective — the construction stays the same!

| Type                         | Example                 |
| ---------------------------- | ----------------------- |
| Komparativ + **än**          | ser äld**re** ut **än** |
| **lika** + positiv + **som** | ser lika gaml**a** ut   |

---

## 🧠 Quick Cheat Card

```
[subject] + ser + [adjective] + ut

en-word  → ser sympatisk ut
ett-word → ser trevligt ut
plural   → ser intressanta ut
```

---

## ⚡ Bonus — ser...ut vs. är

| 👁️ Appearance            | 💎 Reality       |
| ----------------------- | --------------- |
| Han **ser** rik **ut**. | Han **är** rik. |
| *He looks rich.*        | *He is rich.*   |

> Swedish gossip starter pack: **ser ... ut** 😏
